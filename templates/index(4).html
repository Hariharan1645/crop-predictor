INDIA BUDGET India unveils long-term programmes to boost pulses, cotton output

In the Union Budget, Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman announced a six-year program to increase pulse production, aiming to reduce dependency on imports. This involves state agencies procuring crops like pigeon peas, black matpe, and red lentils at guaranteed prices. India, being the largest global importer of pulses, spent a record $5 billion on imports in 2024. The program also focuses on enhancing cotton production, particularly the extra-long staple variety, through research and development efforts.
